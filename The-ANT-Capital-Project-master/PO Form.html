Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is Purchase Order Funding?
Purchase Order Funding is a financial solution that provides businesses with the necessary capital to fulfill large purchase orders by covering production and delivery costs before the customer payment is received.
2. Who is eligible for Purchase Order Funding?
We fund SMMEs awarded supply and delivery purchase orders by government institutions in KwaZulu-Natal and Eastern Cape, needing R35 000 or more. The institution must be willing to enter into a deed of cession agreement.
3. What documents are required to apply for PO funding?
To apply, you'll need:

• A valid Purchase Order or Letter of Appointment with project specifications
• Supplier Quote addressed to The Ant Capital
• Latest Central Supplier Database (CSD) Report with login details
• ID copies of Directors

It’s advisable to confirm with your client if they are willing to enter into a deed of cession agreement before applying.
4. Who can benefit from PO Finance?
PO Finance is ideal for small to medium-sized businesses experiencing cash flow challenges when fulfilling large orders. It helps businesses maintain growth without financial strain and allows them to secure more purchase orders.
5. Do you charge VAT on your services?
Yes, The Ant Capital is a registered VAT vendor, and we charge VAT on all taxable goods and services as per South African tax laws.
6. Do I need good credit for PO Funding?
Not necessarily. The money advanced is specifically for delivering a purchase order. The value of a valid, successfully delivered PO secures the advance. We consider your credit history to assess risk, but our open-minded model focuses more on the PO itself.
